Overview:

The marching arts is a world that is ruled by performers ability to utilize visual appealing performances to communicate a message to an audience. To pull off such shows, many of the elite ensembles worldwide turn to specific companies because they are known to provide the best equipment to groups at the top of the activity. For Color Guard, Designs By King has proven to be a supplier of champions. Within the past two Winter Guard International seasons, nearly every single Independent World Class Finalist has proudly spun their rifles and sabres. Despite this dominanace, however, the website where performers go to either learn more about their products or purchase their own, leaves more to be desired.     • Current Problems

    Before attempting to redeisng an Product page, it is important to first fully grasp it's existing issues. I had used Designs by Kings' product page, years ago and remember experiencing difficulty navigating the site. Returning the webpage after many years, I noticed that some of the issues I noticed long ago were still present, along with new ones evidenced by interacting with the interface on multiple devices, with different screen sizes. I summarized my main comments of the current of the website in the table below.

    Category Descriptions
    Learnability
    • Assumes color guard background
    • Name of website is not clearly shown to the user
    • Hyperlinks and clickable componenets are evident due to underlining of hyperlinked text
    Memorability
    • Based on the current status of the navigation bar, a customer would assume they are on the homepage rather than the product page.
    • The webpage is also pretty memorable, as everything is clearly labeled
    Efficiency
    • Interaction-wise, users use this page to reach other web pages within the site; however, if they return to this page after visiting another a video pops up each time that the user has to then click out of
    • The webpage is also pretty memorable, as everything is clearly labeled
    Concept Model
    • The user would expect the Home Page and Product page to be different. currently, clicking the Home Tab just makes a video appear with the Product Page in the background
    Accessibility and Other Critiques
    • The webpage is missing alternative text for its images
    • The page is not visually appealing and has many instances of hard to read text due to background images
    • The webpage is not responsive to different screen sizes

    In summary, the Product Page of the Designs by King website is fairly usable for any person with a background in color guard or the marching arts. Similarly, the website makes it evident to the which components are interactive and is fairly memorable due to the clear labeling throughout the page. However, the current design could easily confuse a user, specifically regarding the difference between the Home and the Product page. Similarly, it could greatly benefit from a visual redesign that makes it clear to the reader what website they are on. Lastly, the existing website is not responsive, so my redesign must account for that as well.

    Conclusion

    The redesigned version of the Designs by King Product Page provides a more visually appealing experience for the user and allows for easier navigation on more smaller screens. Though many changes have been made to the looks of the website, certain details, such as the inclusion of customer photos and images of offered products are still present. Through this redesign, I was able to build valuable skills in designing webpages using CSS and HTML, more specifically how to allocate certain visual aspects to specific screen dimensions. Through doing so, I hoped to make this portion of the Designs by King webapge more usuable, helping consumers find the high quality products they need with ease.
